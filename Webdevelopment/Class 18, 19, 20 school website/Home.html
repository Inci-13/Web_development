languages brought to Britain in the mid-5th to 7th centuries AD by Anglo-Saxon migrants from
what is now northwest Germany, southern Denmark and the Netherlands. The Anglo-Saxons
settled in the British Isles from the mid-5th century and came to dominate the bulk of southern
Great Britain. Their language originated as a group
of Ingvaeonic languages which were spoken by the
settlers in England and southern and eastern Scotland in the early Middle Ages, displacing
the Celtic languages, and, possibly, British Latin, that had previously been dominant.
